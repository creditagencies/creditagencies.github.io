PayPal Credit vs. Splitit: Which One Should You Use?

Home / Blog / Blog Details

In today’s fast-paced digital economy, flexible payment solutions are more important than ever. With inflation rising and consumer spending habits shifting, many shoppers are looking for ways to manage their budgets without sacrificing convenience. Two popular options—PayPal Credit and Splitit—offer "buy now, pay later" (BNPL) services, but they work very differently.

Whether you're shopping online for the latest tech gadget or covering unexpected medical bills, choosing the right payment plan can make a big difference. So, which one is better for your needs? Let’s break it down.

How PayPal Credit Works

What Is PayPal Credit?

PayPal Credit is a digital line of credit offered by PayPal in partnership with Synchrony Bank. It functions like a virtual credit card, allowing users to make purchases and pay them off over time.

Key Features

  • Interest-Free Period: If you pay the full balance within 6 months on purchases of $99 or more, you won’t incur interest.
  • Revolving Credit: Unlike traditional installment plans, PayPal Credit doesn’t lock you into fixed payments.
  • Wide Acceptance: Works anywhere PayPal is accepted, including major retailers like eBay, Walmart, and Best Buy.
  • Late Fees: Miss a payment? You’ll face a $40 late fee and potential interest charges.

Pros & Cons

✅ No upfront costs if paid within the promo period.
✅ Flexible repayment—pay as little as the minimum due.
❌ High APR (up to 29.99%) if you carry a balance past the promo period.
❌ Credit check required, which may impact your score.

How Splitit Works

What Is Splitit?

Splitit takes a different approach by splitting your purchase into interest-free monthly installments using your existing credit card—no new application needed.

Key Features

  • No Credit Check: Uses your current credit limit, so approval is instant.
  • Interest-Free: Unlike PayPal Credit, Splitit never charges interest.
  • Merchant-Dependent: Only works with retailers that partner with Splitit (e.g., Microsoft, Lenovo).
  • No Fees (Usually): Some merchants may add a small processing fee.

Pros & Cons

✅ No hard credit pull—great for those avoiding new credit inquiries.
✅ True 0% APR if your card supports it.
❌ Limited merchant availability compared to PayPal.
❌ Requires available credit—your card must have enough balance.

PayPal Credit vs. Splitit: Head-to-Head Comparison

1. Approval Process

  • PayPal Credit: Requires a credit check (hard inquiry) and approval from Synchrony Bank.
  • Splitit: No application—just an active credit card with sufficient limit.

Winner: Splitit (if you hate credit checks).

2. Interest & Fees

  • PayPal Credit: 0% APR for 6 months (on $99+), then 29.99% variable APR.
  • Splitit: Always 0% APR, but your card issuer may charge interest if you carry a balance.

Winner: Splitit (if you pay on time).

3. Flexibility

  • PayPal Credit: Lets you pay the minimum or full balance at any time.
  • Splitit: Fixed installments—no early payoff benefit unless your card allows it.

Winner: PayPal Credit (for adjustable payments).

4. Merchant Availability

  • PayPal Credit: Accepted at millions of online stores.
  • Splitit: Only works with select retailers.

Winner: PayPal Credit (for wider usability).

Which One Should You Choose?

Best for Big Purchases (Tech, Furniture, etc.)

If you’re buying a $1,000+ item and can pay it off in 6 months, PayPal Credit’s 0% promo is a smart choice—just avoid the high APR afterward.

Best for Frequent Shoppers

If you prefer no credit checks and shop at Splitit-friendly stores (e.g., Dell, HP), Splitit keeps things simple.

Best for Credit Score Protection

Since Splitit doesn’t require a hard inquiry, it’s better if you’re applying for a mortgage soon or want to avoid credit dings.

Best for Emergency Expenses

Need to cover a medical bill or car repair fast? PayPal Credit’s instant approval and wide acceptance make it more reliable.

Final Thoughts

The right choice depends on your spending habits, credit situation, and repayment discipline.

  • Want flexibility and broad usability? → PayPal Credit.
  • Prefer no interest and no credit checks? → Splitit.

Both services cater to the growing demand for deferred payments, but understanding their differences ensures you won’t get stuck with surprise fees.

Now, the next time you checkout online, you’ll know exactly which option saves you the most money!
