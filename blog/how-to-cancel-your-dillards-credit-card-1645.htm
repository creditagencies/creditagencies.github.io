How to Cancel Your Dillard's Credit Card

Home / Blog / Blog Details

In today’s fast-paced digital world, managing credit cards can feel like juggling flaming torches—especially when you’re trying to simplify your finances. Whether you’re cutting back on debt, avoiding annual fees, or just decluttering your wallet, canceling a store credit card like Dillard’s can be a smart move. But let’s face it: the process isn’t always straightforward. Between customer service hold music and fine print, it’s easy to feel overwhelmed.

Here’s your step-by-step guide to canceling your Dillard’s credit card smoothly, plus tips to protect your credit score and avoid common pitfalls.

Why Cancel Your Dillard’s Credit Card?

1. High Interest Rates

Store credit cards are notorious for sky-high APRs (often 25% or more). If you’re carrying a balance, you could be throwing money away.

2. Limited Usability

Unlike general-purpose cards, Dillard’s card can only be used at Dillard’s stores or online. Unless you’re a frequent shopper, it’s dead weight.

3. Annual Fees

Some versions of the card charge annual fees. Why pay for a card you barely use?

4. Credit Score Impact

Closing a card can affect your credit utilization ratio. But if you’ve got other cards with higher limits, the hit might be minimal.

Step-by-Step: How to Cancel Your Dillard’s Credit Card

Step 1: Pay Off Your Balance

Before canceling, zero out your balance. You can’t close an account with pending charges or unpaid debt.

  • Log in to your Dillard’s credit card account online.
  • Check for pending transactions (returns take time to process).
  • Pay via the website, mobile app, or by calling customer service.

Pro Tip: Wait for a billing cycle to confirm your balance is truly $0.

Step 2: Redeem Rewards

Got Dillard’s rewards points? Use them or lose them.

  • Log in and check your rewards balance.
  • Redeem for discounts, gift cards, or merchandise.
  • Points often vanish once the account closes.

Step 3: Call Customer Service

Dillard’s doesn’t let you cancel online—you must call.

  • Dial 1-800-347-7443 (Dillard’s Credit Services).
  • Navigate the automated menu (usually press “0” for an agent).
  • Verify your identity (have your card and SSN ready).

Script:
“Hi, I’d like to cancel my Dillard’s credit card. My account number is [X]. Can you confirm my balance is $0?”

Step 4: Get Confirmation

  • Ask for a written confirmation (email or letter) that the account is closed.
  • Save this in case of future billing errors.

Step 5: Cut Up the Card

Once closed, shred the card to prevent fraud. Even inactive accounts can be targeted by scammers.

What Happens to Your Credit Score?

Short-Term Impact

  • Credit Utilization: If this card had a high limit, closing it could increase your overall utilization ratio (bad for your score).
  • Average Account Age: If it’s an older card, closing it might shorten your credit history.

Long-Term Fixes

  • Open a no-fee general-purpose card to offset the limit loss.
  • Keep other accounts active and paid on time.

Watch Out for These Scams!

1. “Retention” Offers

Dillard’s might dangle discounts to keep you. Politely decline if you’re set on closing.

2. Phantom Fees

Some issuers sneak in “closure fees.” Ask upfront: “Are there any fees for closing my account?”

3. Fraudulent Reactivation

Scammers prey on closed accounts. Monitor your credit report (use AnnualCreditReport.com) for odd activity.

Alternatives to Canceling

1. Downgrade to a No-Fee Card

Ask if Dillard’s offers a basic card with no annual fee.

2. Freeze the Card

Temporarily lock the card via the app if you’re unsure about canceling.

3. Negotiate a Better Rate

Threaten to cancel? They might lower your APR.

Final Thought: Financial Freedom Starts Here

Canceling a credit card is like breaking up with a clingy ex—it’s awkward but liberating. By following these steps, you’ll ditch the Dillard’s card without drama and keep your credit score intact. Now go enjoy that clutter-free wallet!
