Best Business Credit Cards for 2024

Home / Blog / Blog Details

The business landscape in 2024 is more dynamic than ever, with inflation, supply chain disruptions, and the rise of remote work reshaping how companies operate. In this environment, choosing the right business credit card can be a game-changer—helping you manage cash flow, earn valuable rewards, and even offset rising costs. Whether you’re a solopreneur, a small business owner, or a corporate executive, here’s a breakdown of the best business credit cards for 2024.

Why a Business Credit Card Matters in 2024

Inflation and Rising Operational Costs

With inflation still a concern, businesses are looking for ways to stretch every dollar. A well-chosen business credit card can offer cash back, travel rewards, or statement credits that help mitigate higher expenses.

The Shift to Hybrid Work

As remote and hybrid work models persist, expenses like home office setups, co-working memberships, and digital tools are on the rise. The right card can reward these purchases.

Supply Chain and Global Commerce

Businesses navigating international transactions need cards with low foreign transaction fees and robust travel benefits to support frequent logistics adjustments.

Top Business Credit Cards for 2024

Best All-Around Card: Chase Ink Business Preferred®

  • Annual Fee: $95
  • Key Perks:
    • 3X points on travel, shipping, internet/cable/phone services, and advertising (up to $150K annually)
    • 25% bonus when redeeming points through Chase Ultimate Rewards®
    • Cell phone protection up to $1K per claim

Why It Stands Out:
With strong rewards on common business expenses and flexible redemption options, this card is ideal for growing businesses.

Best for Cash Back: Capital One Spark Cash Plus

  • Annual Fee: $150 (waived first year)
  • Key Perks:
    • Unlimited 2% cash back on all purchases
    • $1K cash bonus after spending $30K in the first 3 months
    • No preset spending limit

Why It Stands Out:
Perfect for businesses that prefer straightforward cash rewards without category restrictions.

Best for Travel: American Express® Business Platinum Card

  • Annual Fee: $695
  • Key Perks:
    • 5X points on flights and prepaid hotels via Amex Travel
    • Airport lounge access (Centurion, Priority Pass, etc.)
    • Up to $400 annual Dell statement credit

Why It Stands Out:
High annual fee but unmatched travel perks, making it ideal for frequent flyers.

Best for Startups: Brex Corporate Card

  • Annual Fee: $0
  • Key Perks:
    • No personal guarantee required
    • Points on every purchase (7X on rideshare, 4X on travel, etc.)
    • Integrates with accounting software like QuickBooks

Why It Stands Out:
A no-fee, high-reward card designed for tech startups and venture-backed businesses.

Best for International Spending: Bank of America® Business Advantage Travel Rewards

  • Annual Fee: $0
  • Key Perks:
    • 1.5X points on all purchases
    • No foreign transaction fees
    • Flexible redemption for flights, hotels, or cash back

Why It Stands Out:
A cost-effective option for businesses with global operations.

How to Choose the Right Card for Your Business

Assess Your Spending Patterns

  • Do you spend more on travel, office supplies, or digital services?
  • Are you frequently making international transactions?

Consider Fees vs. Benefits

  • A high annual fee may be justified if the rewards offset it.
  • Look for waived fees in the first year to test the card.

Evaluate Redemption Flexibility

  • Some cards offer higher value when points are transferred to airline/hotel partners.
  • Others provide simple cash back or statement credits.

Emerging Trends in Business Credit Cards

AI-Powered Spending Insights

Many issuers now offer AI-driven analytics to track expenses and optimize budgets—helpful in uncertain economic times.

Sustainability-Focused Rewards

Cards like the Amex Green Business Card incentivize eco-friendly purchases with bonus points.

Enhanced Security Features

With cyber threats rising, look for cards offering virtual card numbers and advanced fraud protection.

Final Tips for 2024

  • Negotiate with Issuers: Some banks may waive fees or offer higher sign-up bonuses if you have strong credit.
  • Monitor Policy Changes: Rewards structures and fees can shift—stay updated to maximize value.
  • Leverage Sign-Up Bonuses: Many cards offer lucrative intro bonuses, but meet spending requirements wisely.

The right business credit card in 2024 isn’t just about rewards—it’s a strategic tool to navigate economic headwinds and fuel growth. Choose wisely, and your card could become one of your most valuable business assets.
